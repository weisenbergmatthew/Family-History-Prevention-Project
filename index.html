A family history you'll actually use

The strongest early warning about your health is sitting in your family's medical history.

Most people have never written it down. This tool walks you through gathering it — then shows which conditions your history points to most strongly, and turns that into a concise, guideline-based plan to help prevent them.

Why we ask for ages

Add ages when you can

A young diagnosis points to inherited genes; the same disease later in life usually reflects lifestyle and environment. Ages help us separate genetic risk from environmental — and pull screening earlier. Leave blank if unknown.
